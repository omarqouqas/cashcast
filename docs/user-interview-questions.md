# User Interview Questions

**Goal:** Understand who CashCast is actually for, what specific problem it solves, and whether that problem is painful enough to pay for.

**Format:** 20-30 minute calls. Let them talk. Follow the energy.

---

## Opening (2 min)

> "Thanks for chatting. I'm trying to understand how freelancers manage their money — not to sell you anything, just to learn. There are no wrong answers."

---

## Part 1: Context & Current Situation (5 min)

1. **Tell me about your freelance work.** What do you do? How long have you been doing it?

2. **How would you describe your income pattern?** (Let them use their own words — irregular, unpredictable, feast-or-famine, etc.)

3. **Walk me through how you currently keep track of your finances.** (Spreadsheets? Apps? Mental math? Nothing?)

---

## Part 2: Pain Discovery (10 min)

4. **Think back to the last time you felt stressed about money. What was happening?**
   - Follow up: How did you handle it?

5. **Have you ever been surprised by a bill you forgot about, or realized you couldn't afford something you thought you could?**
   - Follow up: What happened? How did it make you feel?

6. **When you're about to make a purchase — say, $500+ — how do you decide if you can afford it?**
   - Follow up: How confident are you in that decision?

7. **What's the most frustrating part of managing money with irregular income?**
   - Follow up: How often does this come up?

8. **Have you ever turned down work or a purchase because you weren't sure about your cash situation?**

---

## Part 3: Current Solutions (5 min)

9. **What tools or methods have you tried to get a handle on this?**
   - Follow up: What worked? What didn't?

10. **If you use spreadsheets or apps, how much time do you spend on them per week?**

11. **What would need to change for you to feel financially "in control"?**

---

## Part 4: Value & Willingness to Pay (5 min)

12. **If I told you there was a tool that could show you your bank balance 90 days from now — would that be useful?**
    - Follow up: Why or why not?

13. **What would that be worth to you? Would you pay $5/month? $10? $20?**
    - Follow up: What would it need to do to be worth that?

14. **What would make you NOT use a tool like this?** (Privacy? Complexity? Already solved?)

---

## Part 5: Closing (3 min)

15. **Is there anything I didn't ask that you think I should know?**

16. **Do you know any other freelancers who struggle with this? Could you intro me?** (Referral for more interviews)

---

## Interviewer Notes

**Listen for:**
- Emotional language ("stressed," "anxious," "surprised," "scrambling")
- Frequency of the problem ("every month," "constantly," "once in a while")
- Current workarounds and their pain points
- Specific dollar amounts and scenarios
- Whether they've tried to solve this before (indicates real pain)

**Red flags (may not be ideal customer):**
- "I just check my bank app" (problem not painful enough)
- "My accountant handles it" (different market)
- "I have a steady retainer so it's predictable" (doesn't have the core problem)

**Green flags (likely ideal customer):**
- Uses words like "anxiety," "guessing," "surprised"
- Has tried spreadsheets but they got stale
- Mentions specific scenarios of running low
- Asks "wait, does this tool exist?" before you explain

---

## After the Interview

Log answers in a spreadsheet with columns:
- Name / Role
- Income pattern description (their words)
- Primary pain point
- Current solution
- Would they pay? How much?
- Notable quotes
- Referrals given

After 10 interviews, patterns will emerge. After 20, you'll know who this is for.
